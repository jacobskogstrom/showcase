Proposal for Wekudata AB

Infrastructure you already trust. An AI operator people will actually use.

A pilot to combine Wekudata's managed hosting with The Pocket Operator — a private, per-customer AI environment built on infrastructure you guarantee.

Good talking over lunch, Artur. Wanted to put the idea we sketched into something concrete you can react to — not a pitch deck, just the plan.

The gap

AI tools people don't trust, on infrastructure nobody's accountable for

Every customer you host for already trusts Wekudata with their infrastructure — 25+ years of cloud, hosting, and managed networks. What most of them don't have is an AI layer on top of that trust: something a whole team can actually use day to day, running on infrastructure someone accountable manages, not a black-box SaaS in someone else's cloud.

The pairing

Wekudata's infrastructure. The Pocket Operator's product.

Wekudata guarantees the IT infrastructure and hosting: each customer gets their own private environment — email, tasks, files, calendar — hosted and managed the way you already do it. The Pocket Operator runs on top of that: a personal AI operator per person or team, reachable from Telegram, that reads and writes their notes, todos, calendar, and email.

🔒 Wekudata brings

The infrastructure guarantee — hosting, backup, access control, data residency — plus the existing customer relationships and support capacity to back it.

⚙️ Pocket Operator brings

The AI-operator product itself, and hands-on coaching so a team actually adopts it — not just a login, a new way of working.

What it looks like in use

The way I work with it, every day

This proposal itself is a good example: I dictated the outline for it from the car on the way home, sent it as a task, and it came back done. That's the habit I'd coach a customer's team into — add a task, send an email, capture a note, hands-free, from wherever they are. No app to open, no dashboard to learn. Just a message.

How we get there

Steps to secure and test this

1

Pick the pilot ground

Either internally at Wekudata, or one small, friendly customer you already trust — whichever gives us the fastest honest read.

2

Define the infra guarantee

One page: what Wekudata commits to for a private environment — hosting, backup, access control, data residency. Your team owns this spec.

3

Stand up the environment

I set up The Pocket Operator on that infrastructure and run a short onboarding session with the pilot team — how to add a task, send an email, ask for a summary.

4

Run it for real, 2–4 weeks

Real tasks, real email, real usage — not a demo. I coach along the way as questions come up.

5

Check in together

Review what worked, what didn't, and whether it's worth extending to more of your customers.

6

Formalize the terms

Once the pilot proves out, we lock the commercial model down properly.

Commercial model
