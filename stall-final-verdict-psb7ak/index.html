What happened — the whole journey

2026-07-06 · the problem

Engine died twice at sea — zero warning

No fault on the ICON display either time; instant clean restart on a key cycle. That silence was the key clue: a monitored fault would have displayed. Something was cutting the EMM's 12V power itself.

2026-07-10 → 07-13 · the diagnosis

EvDiag6 pull nails it: battery-side power loss

Fault cluster at engine hour 219:39 — codes 94 (fuel pump current), 114 (network loss, battery at 0,5V while running), 161 (Fused B), 79 (starter solenoid) — all with battery voltage collapsed while the engine ran on its own 55V magneto rail. Helm logged matching P32 "Accessory power disconnected". Dockside datalog then caught it live: 15V → 3,4V collapse, 51A recovery spike, at idle, at the dock. Battery/ground path confirmed; kill-switch and ignition ruled out.

2026-07-16 · fix #1

New batteries + new pole connectors

2× Biltema AGM 92 Ah / 800 A(EN) (spec minimum 675 CCA — comfortable margin), CTEK-charged before install. Old pole connectors had water-ingress corrosion. Same-day EvDiag6 session: zero new fault codes, textbook charging (12,6→14,2V, 8–19A taper), plus a ~1h14m hard run to 5 500 RPM logged clean. A filmed "voltage drift" (12,3–12,8V, key on) was analyzed and closed as benign key-on load cycling.

2026-07-18 · fix #2 — the smoking gun

Two loose cables found behind the battery switch

On the backside of the Blue Sea 6007 selector: two loose cables on the OUTPUT/COM stud — the single point feeding the whole boat-side 12V. Retightened to the 120 in·lb spec; cracked insulation boots on studs 1/2 flagged for re-shrink. Pre/post photos archived. This one part explains the stalls, all four fault codes, the helm P32, and the steering-whine voltage sag.

2026-07-18 evening · verification #2

Full instrumented re-test — clean

EMM report at 223:40 h: still zero new codes since 219:39. Two live datalogs: battery 12,8–13,5V, charge 10–21A, system 52–57V, no dropout. Filmed multimeter vs ICON gauge: they agree (13,0V key-off → 12,3–12,9V key-on band). DPS steering test on the EvDiag6 Power Steering screen: lock-to-lock ±31,8°, pressures alternating correctly, pump current spiking normally under load — no visible 12V sag during steering anymore.

Verdict

Yes — the findings are good enough to keep boating this season. The failure was electrical power delivery, not engine internals (temps, knock, compression indicators all clean). The root cause was found physically, matches the data signature exactly, and the repair has been verified by instrumented re-testing. Even the worst-case recurrence mode is a momentary power blip with instant restart — annoying, not dangerous — and the engine's magneto keeps it running independently of the battery.

Conditions attached:

  • Book the Lomma service promptly — the impeller/water pump is a real seaworthiness item (engine built 2016; 500 h / 5 yr interval applies) — don't let it slide to winter.
  • Hard rule stays: never turn the battery selector while the engine runs (taped reminder recommended — kids aboard).
  • Two 5-minute checks at the next calm dock visit: mA parasitic test at the helm socket (the 0,5V ghost), and the engine-idling wiggle test on cables/switch while watching live voltage.
  • Keep the multimeter + a quart of XD100 aboard; watch the ICON battery gauge on the first few longer trips.
